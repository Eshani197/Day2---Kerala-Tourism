Kerala

Kerala, also known as God's Own Country, is on the southwestern coast of India. The state is home to the world-famous backwaters, most beautiful beaches of India, the highest peak in the Western Ghats & South India, many of the old temples of India, and much more.

Districts

Kerala, a state in southern India, is divided into 14 districts, each with its own unique charm and attractions. From the bustling city of Thiruvananthapuram to the serene backwaters of Alappuzha, Kerala's districts offer a diverse range of experiences. Explore the rich cultural heritage of Thrissur, the picturesque hill stations of Idukki, and the vibrant markets of Kozhikode. Whether you're interested in history, nature, or cuisine, Kerala's districts have something special to offer every traveler. Each district is a treasure trove of natural beauty, cultural diversity, and historical significance, waiting to be explored.
Thiruvananthapuram

Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city of Kerala. The district Located in the southwestern tip of India and is noted for internationally renowned beaches, historic monuments, backwater stretches and a rich cultural heritage and is a major center for art and culture in Kerala.

Kollam

Kollam was one of the leading trade capitals of the Old World. This district is the centre of the country’s cashew trading and processing industry. The district’s Ashtamudi Lake is the gateway to the magnificent backwaters of Kerala

Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta – a district is noted for its temples, rivers, mountain ranges, water fiestas, and heritage villages like Aranmula and Vasthu Vidya Gurukulam. The district is home to the world famous Aranmula Kannadi or metal mirrors.

Know More
Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is famous for its backwaters and houseboat cruises. The district is often referred to as the "Venice of the East" due to its intricate network of canals and lagoons.

Know More
Kottayam

Kottayam district is the nerve centre of Kerala’s newspaper industry. The majestic Vembanad Lake and the stunning backwaters of Kumarakom are its prime attractions. The district is also noted for Christian missionaries.

Know More
Idukki

Idukki is known for its stunning hills, spice plantations, and wildlife sanctuaries. The district is home to the famous Idukki Dam and the picturesque hill station of Munnar.

Know More
Ernakulam

Ernakulam district is considered to be one of the business metropolises of India. A fascinating mixture of the old and the new, the district comprises many interesting cities including Kochi (Cochin) the Commercial Capital of Kerala

Know More
Thrissur

Thrissur district, cultural capital of Kerala, is noted for its Pooram Festival. The district is also the abode of several cultural centres like Kerala Kalamandalam, Sahitya Academy and the Sangeetha Nataka Academy

Know More
Palakkad

Palakkad district is known as the Granary of Kerala. It is a vast expanse of verdant plains interspersed with hills, rivers, mountain streams and forests. This district is also famous for its traditional art forms and festivals.

Know More
Malappuram

Malappuram has a rich and eventful history. The district is a famous centre for Hindu-Vedic learning and Islamic philosophy, the temples and mosques of this region are well known for their spectacular festivals.

Know More
Kozhikode

Kozhikode district in Kerala is one of the most important regions of the Malabar Coast. Serene beaches, historic sites, wildlife sanctuaries, rivers and hills make Kozhikode a popular destination.

Know More
Wayanad

Wayanad is a bio-diverse district with beautiful hill stations. It is located on the southern tip of the magnificent Deccan plateau in Kerala. The district is known for its lush green landscapes, wildlife sanctuaries, and rich cultural heritage.

Know More
Kannur

Bounded by the Western Ghats in the east and the Lakshadweep Sea in the west, Kannur district in Kerala showcases its remarkable history in the form of ancient forts, old shrines and august cultural and educational institutions that dot its scenic landscape.

Know More
Kasaragod

Kasaragod district is home to the largest and best preserved fort in the State, Kerala, India – Bekal. It is also noted for its spectacular art forms of Theyyam, Yakshagana, Poorakkali and Kolkali. It shines with its unique cultural heritage and natural beauty.
